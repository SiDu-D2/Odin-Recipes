Chickpea Tuna

Because fish are friends

Why even call it tuna when there's no fish involved? Because the language helps ease my wife into a false sense of security which is a great place for her to be as it's actually the only place where she'll actually eat it.

Be warned, highly technical recipe ahead.

Ingredients

  • Chickpeas
  • Mayonnaise
  • Seaweed (wtf?)
  • Salt n pepper
  • Crunchy veg (optional)
  • Bread (optional, but mandatory if making a sandwich)

Methodology

  1. Drain chickpeas from can or cook them from their dried beginnings.
  2. Mash 'em. Use a fork, use a masher, use your hands. Just mash 'em.
  3. Mix with everything else.
  4. Eat it.

Notes

I don't recommend trying to fool anyone into thinking it's real tuna. I also don't recommend having this with pasta and hoping it'll be like tuna pasta, because it won't and you'll regret it.
